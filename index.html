Abstract thoughts spoken with street talk, a man wearing track suit pants reading Italian vogue: Jimmy Hawk's latest single 'On the Border' will leave you re-imaging the suburbs.

Abstract thoughts spoken with street talk, a man wearing track suit pants reading Italian vogue: Jimmy Hawk's latest single 'On the Border' will leave you re-imaging the suburbs.

Jimmy Hawk is one of Australia's most understated singer-songwriters. Throughout 5 albums, Hawk has continuously blurred genres, with each release turning expectations on its head. The evolution of the new single, 'On the Border' is his most mature and wild yet.

Written in collaboration with Michael Farell, one of Australia's most celebrated contemporary poets, the pair have tapped into a rare breed of songwriting that portrays the underdog and heroic. Like a Smiths or a Bob Dylan song, 'On the Border' plays on multitudes: absurdism, rawness, absence and intima­cy. Combining the dreamy sounds of the War On Drugs with the intrigue of Lee Hazlewood, listening to 'On the Border' feels like your walking through a carnival at sunset.

Three years on from 2017's Young Hysteria album, Jimmy Hawk reminds you why the everyday can be more mysterious than it is familiar. Listen to On the Border.
